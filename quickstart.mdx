This walkthrough takes you from zero to enforced SSO for your organization. The exact provider steps depend on whether you're connecting Okta, Google Workspace, or a generic SAML / OIDC IdP — each has its own page.

## Prerequisites

- You're an org admin in Mintie
- You have admin access in your IdP (Okta, Google Workspace, etc.)

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open SSO settings">
    Navigate to **Settings → Auth → SSO** in the Mintie dashboard.

    <Note>
      The admin UI is in development. Until it ships, your account team
      will configure your IdP connection on your behalf.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your IdP">
    Pick your provider and follow the provider-specific guide:

    - [Okta](/setup/okta)
    - [Google Workspace](/setup/google-workspace)
    - [Generic SAML / OIDC](/setup/generic-saml)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a test sign-in">
    The admin UI surfaces a "Test sign-in" link that triggers a real
    round-trip to your IdP. Use it before enforcing SSO — it's the safest
    way to catch claim mapping or attribute issues.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enforce SSO">
    Flip **Require SSO** for the org. Active password sessions are migrated
    to SSO via the [session-migration job](/concepts/sessions).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify your users">
    Mintie automatically emails password users at T-30, T-7, and T-1 days
    before their password stops working. See [user comms](/migration/user-comms).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What changes for your users

Once SSO is enforced:

- The login screen routes users to your IdP after they enter their email
- Existing browser sessions are forced to re-auth via SSO within minutes
- Password reset flows are disabled for SSO-required orgs
- API keys remain valid until [tied to IdP identity](/api-and-keys/api-keys) ships
