Intuit reports four operating segments. Each has its own GM, P&L, and engineering org.

## Small Business & Self-Employed (SBSE)

The biggest segment by revenue. Powers QuickBooks, Mailchimp, Payroll, Payments, and our mid-market product.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Customers" icon="users">~9M global QBO subscribers, ~13M Mailchimp users</Card>
  <Card title="Strategic bet" icon="trending-up">"Done-for-you" books with embedded AI</Card>
</CardGroup>

- [Payroll](/products/quickbooks/payroll)
- [Mailchimp overview](/products/mailchimp/overview)

## Consumer Group

Owns TurboTax — including DIY, Live Assisted, and Live Full Service. Also handles tax data partnerships and seasonal infrastructure.

<Tip>
  TurboTax revenue is **highly seasonal** — peaks January through April. Engineering capacity planning revolves around the [seasonal readiness program](/products/turbotax/seasonal-readiness).
</Tip>

## Credit Karma

Wholly-owned subsidiary, runs on its own infrastructure but shares the Intuit identity layer. See [Intuit ID](/products/cross/intuit-id).

- Revenue model: lender-paid offer placements + Credit Karma Money (deposit/spend)
- ~140M members, ~70M monthly actives in the U.S. and Canada

## ProTax

Smallest segment by revenue but high margin. Tools for accounting and tax professionals: Lacerte, ProConnect Tax Online, ProSeries, and EasyACCT.

ProTax shares engineering DNA with TurboTax — the [tax engine](/products/turbotax/tax-engine) is the same.
