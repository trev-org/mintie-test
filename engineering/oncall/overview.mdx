If you build a service, you run it. On-call is how we make that real.

## Rotation shape

Most teams use a two-tier rotation:

| Tier            | Coverage              | Response time | Notes                                          |
| --------------- | --------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Primary         | 1 week shift, 24/7    | 5 min ack     | Pages on alerts; first response                |
| Secondary       | 1 week shift, 24/7    | 15 min ack    | Backup if primary doesn't ack                  |
| Manager-on-call | 2 week shift          | 30 min        | Coordinates if pages cluster or escalate       |

Follow-the-sun teams (Payments, Identity) split rotations across MV / ATL / BLR / TLV so no one sleeps through pages.

## Compensation

- **Primary on-call**: $1,000 per week stipend (US), country-equivalent elsewhere
- **Secondary**: $300 per week
- **Manager-on-call**: $500 per shift
- **Pages outside business hours that result in real work**: extra hour of comp for each hour worked, up to 8 per night
- **Holiday on-call**: 1.5× stipend during major holidays

## When to page someone

Page when:

- A service is down or degrading customer experience
- A security incident is suspected
- A data integrity issue is detected
- A compliance gate is at risk

Don't page for:

- Issues with no customer impact and a clear next-business-day window
- Curiosity about behavior — file a ticket
- Internal-tooling issues unless they block on-call response

## Escalation paths

If you can't reach the responsible team:

1. Service's secondary on-call (PagerDuty)
2. Service's manager-on-call
3. Domain SRE on-call
4. ICP platform on-call (if platform-related)
5. Engineering leadership on-call (last resort, sev-1 only)

The Service Catalog lists each path per service.

## Pages don't follow you off rotation

When your shift ends, your phone goes silent. Hand off via the **on-call sync** with the next shift, covering open issues, recent changes, and known landmines.

## Healthy on-call

A healthy rotation has:

- < 3 sev-3+ pages per week
- < 1 after-hours page per week per engineer
- 95%+ ack within 5 minutes
- Postmortems for every sev-1 and most sev-2

If your rotation isn't healthy, it's a tech-debt signal. Talk to your manager — fixing rotations is real work.
