Postmortems exist to **learn**, not to assign blame. We've never fired anyone for an honest mistake. We have fired people for hiding mistakes.

## When required

| Severity | Postmortem required | Timeline                       |
| -------- | ------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Sev-1    | Always              | Draft in 5 business days       |
| Sev-2    | Default yes         | Draft in 10 business days      |
| Sev-3    | Per team discretion | If yes, draft in 10 days       |
| Sev-4    | No                  | —                              |
| Security | Always (SEC-1, SEC-2)| Draft in 5 business days       |

## Template

```markdown
# Postmortem: <short title>

## TL;DR
One sentence: what happened, what was the customer impact.

## Impact
- Customer impact (count, % of users, dollars)
- Internal impact (engineering hours, on-call burden)
- Compliance / reporting impact (if any)

## Timeline
ISO timestamps + UTC. Cover: detection, escalation, mitigation, recovery.

## What happened
A narrative, in chronological order, written for someone unfamiliar.

## Why it happened
Use the **5 whys** or similar. Surface contributing factors:
- Technical
- Process
- Cultural
- Tools

## What went well
At least three things. We'd like to keep doing them.

## What went poorly
At least three things. Be specific and concrete.

## Where we got lucky
Factors that prevented this from being worse and that we can't rely on next time.

## Action items
| # | Owner | Description | Priority | Due |

## Lessons learned
Reusable takeaways that other teams should hear.
```

## Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Author the draft">
    Usually the IC or the most-involved engineer. Ask reviewers to challenge facts and timing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review meeting">
    Open meeting, anyone can attend. The point is shared learning, not approval. Sev-1 PMs are reviewed in the **PM Forum** — a fortnightly meeting where engineering leadership reviews recent incidents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Action items">
    Track in Jira. Postmortems with no closed action items 30 days later get escalated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish">
    Postmortems are published to the company-wide repo of `intuit/postmortems`. Sev-1s linked from `#eng-postmortems`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Blamelessness in practice

Replace "Alice deployed a bad config" with "the config validation step did not catch the bad value." The action item then targets the system, not Alice.

If the system genuinely allowed a single human to cause an outage, that's the bug to fix.

## Action item discipline

Action items have:

- A **named owner** (a person, not a team)
- A **clear definition of done**
- A **due date** that's realistic (90 days max)
- Priority

We track action item closure rate as an engineering health metric. Open items past due raise flags weekly.
