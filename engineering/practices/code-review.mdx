Code review is a primary place where engineering culture happens. Treat it accordingly.

## Reviewer turnaround

| Author              | Reviewer expectation                  |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| New hire            | < 4 business hours                    |
| Anyone else         | Same business day, < 1 business day after |
| Late-Friday PR      | Monday morning is fine                |
| Sev-1 hotfix        | Drop everything                       |

If you can't review within a day, **decline or reassign** quickly so the PR finds a reviewer who can.

## Author expectations

- **PR description** explains *why*, not just *what*. Link the ticket.
- **Small PRs**. Median is ~150 lines. Aim for under 400 lines diff.
- **Self-review first**. Read your own PR before requesting review; you'll catch a lot.
- **Test plan** — what you tested, how, what you didn't. Screenshots for UI.
- **No drive-by changes** — refactoring + new feature in the same PR is harder to review.
- **Use draft PRs** for early feedback when shape is unclear.

## Reviewer expectations

- **Review the design, not just the code.** Wrong design > nice code.
- **Be specific.** "This is confusing" → "I'd rename `processData` to `splitOrders` so callers know what it does."
- **Be timely.** A 2-hour bottleneck on review can derail someone's day.
- **Accept "good enough."** Perfect is the enemy of shipped.
- **Ask, don't demand.** "What do you think about X?" leaves room for the author's reasoning.

## Approval rules

- Two reviewers required, one from CODEOWNERS
- One approval is the floor; complex changes deserve more
- Authors do not approve their own PRs (yes, it's checked)
- Force-merging requires written justification posted to `#eng-norms`

## When to escalate

If you and a reviewer can't agree:

1. Discuss synchronously (call > thread)
2. Bring in a third opinion (often a tech lead)
3. Document the tradeoff in an [ADR](/engineering/practices/architecture-decisions)
4. Last resort: tech lead or staff+ resolves

## Bots and automation

- `auto-merge` allowed when all required checks pass and at least one human approves
- `dependabot` PRs auto-merge for patch versions, manual for minor/major
- Renovate / pnpm-up handles cross-repo dependency consistency
