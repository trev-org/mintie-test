## Why JIT

SCIM is the right answer when your IdP supports it. For teams whose IdP doesn't have SCIM tooling — or who don't want to maintain a SCIM provisioning agent — JIT is the alternative: a user is created the first time they successfully sign in via SSO.

## How it will work

When `jit_enabled = true` for an org and an OIDC callback succeeds for an unknown user, Mintie:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Reads SAML / OIDC attributes from the assertion">
    Email, name, and (optionally) role-mapping claims.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Creates the user with the mapped attributes">
    Default role is `member` unless a role-mapping claim says otherwise.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Links the IdP identity in the same transaction">
    The user is signed in immediately — no second redirect.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Default attribute mapping

| Mintie field | SAML attribute | OIDC claim |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `email` | `email` or `emailAddress` | `email` |
| `firstName` | `firstName` or `givenName` | `given_name` |
| `lastName` | `lastName` or `familyName` | `family_name` |
| `role` | `role` (org-defined) | custom claim |

## When JIT is the wrong choice

- You need deprovisioning (JIT only handles provisioning — use SCIM)
- You need group-based access changes after sign-in (use SCIM)
- Your IdP can't be trusted to send accurate attributes (use manual invite)
