QuickBooks Payments lets QBO customers accept card and ACH payments from their customers, integrated tightly with invoicing and the accounting engine.

It's a B2B2C product: Intuit sells to the SMB; the SMB transacts with their end customer.

## Acceptance methods

- **Online** via invoice "Pay now" email link
- **In-app** via QuickBooks Mobile (manual entry or Tap to Pay)
- **In-store** via card readers (Bluetooth + Lightning, sold via QuickBooks)
- **Recurring** for subscriptions and retainers
- **Hosted checkout** for non-QuickBooks businesses

## Architecture

QuickBooks Payments sits on top of the [Payments Platform](/engineering/services/payments-platform). We add:

- Invoice-aware metadata propagation
- Auto-reconcile to the customer's bank deposit
- Posting to the [accounting engine](/products/quickbooks/accounting-engine)
- Dispute / chargeback workflow integrated with invoicing
- 1099-K reporting for our customers (we issue the form)

## Settlement

Funds settle to the customer's bank account:

- ACH: 2-5 business days from authorization
- Card (standard): next business day
- Card (instant deposit, premium): same day, 1.5% fee

## Risk and underwriting

Every QuickBooks Payments customer goes through KYB (know-your-business). The risk team reviews:

- Industry codes (some prohibited)
- Volume forecasts
- Beneficial ownership
- Adverse media

We hold funds (delayed settlement) for new accounts in higher-risk industries until volume patterns are established.

## Disputes and chargebacks

When a card chargeback arrives:

1. Notification to QBO customer with evidence-upload UI
2. We forward evidence to the network
3. Customer is responsible for the loss if upheld; we bear loss if our anti-fraud signals failed

We aim to keep our chargeback rate well below network thresholds (Visa: 0.9%; Mastercard: 1.5%).
