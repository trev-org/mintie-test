Every transaction in QuickBooks goes through the **accounting engine** — a double-entry bookkeeping system that's the financial source of truth for our small-business customers.

## Double-entry, in 60 seconds

Every transaction creates entries on **at least two accounts**, debits = credits. Examples:

| Event                   | Debit                | Credit                |
| ----------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------- |
| Customer pays invoice   | Cash                 | Accounts Receivable   |
| Pay vendor bill         | Accounts Payable     | Cash                  |
| Issue invoice           | Accounts Receivable  | Sales Revenue         |
| Buy inventory           | Inventory Asset      | Cash or A/P           |
| Run payroll             | Wages Expense        | Cash + Tax Liabilities|

The engine validates this invariant for every transaction. If a transaction would unbalance the books, it's rejected before it commits.

## Chart of accounts

Each realm has its own **chart of accounts** — a hierarchical taxonomy of where money lives. QuickBooks ships with industry-tailored defaults but customers can customize.

## Transaction types

- **Invoice / Bill** — accrual-side recognition
- **Payment** — cash-side movement
- **Journal entry** — direct debit/credit (advanced users)
- **Adjusting entry** — period-close adjustments
- **Bank feed transaction** — imported, then categorized
- **Transfer** — between owned accounts
- **Inventory adjustment** — quantity + cost

Each type has its own validations and downstream effects.

## Period close and lock

Customers (or their accountants) can **close a period** at month/quarter/year end. After close:

- Transactions in the closed period require a "closed period" override password
- Reports for the period are immutable
- Audit trails preserve every change

We never delete transactions. Voids and edits create new ledger entries that explicitly reverse and replace.

## Multi-currency

A realm can transact in multiple currencies. Exchange rates pull from `xe.com` daily; transactions store both the foreign and home-currency amounts. Period reports translate at user-selectable rates (current, period-end, etc.).

## Tax integration

The engine integrates with the [sales-tax service](#) for jurisdiction-specific tax computation on invoices. For income tax, financial reports flow into TurboTax via [Data sharing](/products/cross/data-sharing) when the customer files.

## Why this matters

If the engine has a bug, **every** customer's books are wrong. Test coverage on the engine is the strictest in the company:

- Reference suite of 50,000+ scenarios
- Bit-for-bit reproducibility of summary reports
- Mutation testing — every conditional flipped to ensure tests catch
- Periodic auditor review (our customers' accountants find bugs we miss)
