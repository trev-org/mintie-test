Audiences are the heart of Mailchimp. Every send, journey, and report references an audience.

## What an audience is

An audience is a list of contacts — typically customers, leads, or subscribers of a Mailchimp customer's business. Each contact has:

- An email address (primary identity)
- Optional: name, address, phone
- Custom merge fields (e.g., signup source, plan tier, last purchase)
- Tags (free-form labels)
- Behavioral history (opens, clicks, purchases)

## Limits

| Tier        | Audiences | Contacts per audience |
| ----------- | --------- | --------------------- |
| Free        | 1         | 500                   |
| Essentials  | 3         | 50,000                |
| Standard    | 5         | 100,000+              |
| Premium     | Unlimited | 200,000+              |

## Segmentation

Customers can segment audiences using:

- Demographic filters (custom fields)
- Behavioral filters (opens, clicks, purchase history)
- Geographic
- Engagement (active, lapsed, dormant)
- Predicted segments (Genos-powered: likely to engage, likely to churn)

Segments are **dynamic** — they update as contacts move in and out. Saved segments can be referenced by campaigns and journeys.

## Contact identity

We use email as the primary identity but also track the contact across:

- Browser cookie (mc_eid)
- Device push token (mobile)
- E-commerce ID (when integrated with Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.)
- Mailchimp's internal contact ID

Identity resolution merges these signals into one contact when a high-confidence match is detected (e.g., email captured on cookie-known device).

## Sources of contacts

Contacts come from:

- Manual adds by the customer
- Signup forms (embed, popup, landing page)
- Imports (CSV, ESP-to-ESP migration)
- API
- Integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce, Square, QuickBooks)

## Compliance and consent

Every contact has a consent record:

- Source of consent (form ID, import note, etc.)
- Date of consent
- Opt-in status (single vs. confirmed)

Mailchimp customers are responsible for legal compliance with their list-building. Mailchimp enforces basics:

- Imports require attestation
- Confirmed opt-in supported (and recommended) for high-deliverability
- One-click unsubscribe in every email
- Unsubscribes honored across all campaigns automatically
