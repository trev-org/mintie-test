Deliverability — landing in the inbox vs. spam — is the most important quality metric in email marketing. Mailchimp's reputation is the result of decades of operational discipline.

## What ISPs care about

Inbox vs. spam routing depends on:

1. **Sender reputation** — IP and domain reputation tracked by ISPs
2. **Authentication** — SPF, DKIM, DMARC alignment
3. **Engagement** — opens, clicks, replies vs. complaints, deletes
4. **Content** — spam-flag patterns in subject and body
5. **List hygiene** — bounces, spam traps, complaint rate
6. **Volume patterns** — sudden volume changes look suspicious

We monitor and tune all of these.

## Reputation metrics

| Metric                                | Healthy threshold      |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Complaint rate                         | < 0.1% (Gmail), < 0.3% overall |
| Bounce rate                            | < 5%                   |
| Spam trap hits                         | 0                      |
| Unsubscribe rate                       | < 0.5%                 |
| Sender Score (Validity)                | > 90                   |
| Inbox placement rate (Litmus)          | > 95%                  |

We alert when any of these regress.

## Authentication

- **SPF** — record customer's sending domains in `v=spf1 include:servers.mcsv.net ~all`
- **DKIM** — Mailchimp signs every email; we encourage customers to authenticate their own domain (CNAME records → our keys)
- **DMARC** — alignment policy at the customer's domain, ideally `p=quarantine` or `p=reject`

## Customer education

Deliverability is partly Mailchimp's job and partly the customer's. Our docs (publicly at mailchimp.com/help) cover:

- List building (don't buy lists)
- Reactivation hygiene (cull dormant addresses)
- Authentication setup
- Sending cadence
- Subject-line norms

Bad customers can hurt good customers via shared IP reputation. We have to enforce.

## Enforcement

Patterns that get accounts suspended or limited:

- Mass-importing purchased lists
- Sudden 10× volume increases without warmup
- Complaint rates above thresholds
- Spam trap hits
- Phishing or scam content

The Trust team handles enforcement, balancing customer growth and ecosystem health.
