Tax season runs roughly **mid-January through mid-April**, with peak day on **April 15**. The full operational cycle:

| Month         | Activity                                                                |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Jul–Sep (off) | Major refactors, year-over-year tax law updates, tooling investments    |
| Oct           | Code freeze planning. Capacity testing begins.                          |
| Nov           | Tax-year rules freeze. Pre-season load tests.                           |
| Dec           | Early-bird filers (W-2 forecasts). Final security review.               |
| Jan           | IRS opens e-file. We open in lockstep. **Daily war room** starts.       |
| Feb–Mar       | Steady-state peak. Live-experts onboarding wave 2.                      |
| Mar 15        | First push: corp/partnership extension deadline.                        |
| Apr 1–14      | Operational lockdown. Only sev-1 fixes. Pre-staged DR drills.           |
| Apr 15        | Peak day. All hands.                                                    |
| Apr 16–18     | Post-peak hot wash. Quick fixes.                                        |
| May–Jun       | Postmortem cycle. Plan for next year.                                   |

## Code freezes

Two freezes:

1. **Pre-season freeze** — December 15 to January 31. Only IRS-clarification patches and critical fixes.
2. **Peak freeze** — April 1 to April 18. Sev-1 hotfixes only. Requires VP approval per change.

During freeze, `intuit deploy` enforces this — pushes are rejected unless tagged `freeze-exempt` with a linked ticket.

## Capacity planning

Capacity is dimensioned against **2× the previous year's peak**. We provision:

- 4× normal compute, scaling to 10× via auto-scaling
- 3× normal database connections
- 2× the normal Stripe / IRS rate limits (negotiated annually)
- Pre-warmed pods to avoid cold-start cliffs

## Live experts staffing

The Live Assisted and Live Full Service businesses scale staff massively during season:

- ~5,000 tax pros at peak (vs. ~500 baseline)
- Onboarding waves in November, January, March
- Capacity-managed by domain (sole proprietor, investor, multistate, etc.)

Engineering supports this with the [Live Experts platform](/products/turbotax/live-experts).

## DR drills

Every February we run two DR drills:

1. **Region failover** — primary region simulated unavailable, traffic shifts to DR
2. **IRS comms outage** — simulated IRS unavailability for 4 hours, queue draining tested

Real outages during peak are very rare; drills are the way we keep capability.

## On-call shape

- Primary on-call rotation expands from 5 engineers to 25 during season
- 24/7 follow-the-sun across MV, ATL, BLR, TLV
- Manager-on-call mandatory; VP-on-call from April 1
