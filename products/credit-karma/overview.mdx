Credit Karma is a wholly-owned subsidiary acquired in 2020. It runs largely on its own infrastructure but federates with Intuit ID for cross-product sign-in.

## What it does

Credit Karma helps members:

- See their credit scores and reports for free, with weekly updates
- Get personalized recommendations for credit cards, loans, and insurance
- Monitor identity and credit changes
- Bank: Credit Karma Money Spend (debit) and Save (HYSA)
- Save and budget tools

Members are largely U.S. consumers; expanding in Canada and the U.K.

## Business model

Credit Karma is **paid by lenders** (and other partners) when members are matched and convert (apply, get approved, originate). Approval-likelihood predictions drive the matching engine — the more accurate the prediction, the more valuable the lead, the higher the payout.

| Vertical                | Revenue                                      |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Credit cards            | Largest single segment                       |
| Personal loans          | High-margin                                  |
| Auto loans              | Growing                                      |
| Mortgage                | Volatile, rate-sensitive                     |
| Insurance               | Auto, home, life                             |
| Banking (Money)         | Spread + interchange                         |

## Member volume (FY25)

- ~140M U.S. members (about half of U.S. adults with credit)
- ~70M monthly actives in U.S.
- ~15M Canadian members
- ~5M U.K. members

## Compliance landscape

- **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)** — we operate as a CRA reseller; covered tightly
- **Equal Credit Opportunity Act / Reg B** — recommendations must not facilitate disparate-impact lending
- **State lending laws** — recommendations are state-aware
- **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)** — frequent regulator
