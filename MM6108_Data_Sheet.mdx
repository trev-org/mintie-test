# MM6108 SoC Data Sheet

Wi-Fi HaLow / IEEE 802.11ah · Sub-1 GHz 1/2/4/8 MHz Bandwidth · MAC/PHY/Radio SoC

---

## 1 Product Overview

### 1.1 Introduction

Wi-Fi HaLow (pronounced HEY-low) is the first global Wi-Fi standard (IEEE 802.11ah) tailored to meet the Internet of Things (IoT) needs. It operates in the sub-1 GHz license-exempt RF bands (850–950 MHz range), enabling longer range, lower power, and lower cost than traditional Wi-Fi.

Morse Micro, the world's leading Wi-Fi HaLow (802.11ah) solutions provider, offers several Wi-Fi HaLow connectivity solutions. The MM6108 SoC is a single-chip solution that includes Radio, PHY, and MAC functions compliant with the IEEE 802.11ah standard and supports data rates up to 32.5 Mbps. The radio supports programmable operation between 850 MHz and 950 MHz.

The MM6108 has been designed for a simplified Wi-Fi HaLow connection to an external host. The RF interface includes the option to use either on-chip amplification for typical low-power, low-cost devices, or an external PCB-mounted power amplifier for ultra-long-reach applications.

### 1.2 Features

**Radio**

- Single-stream max data rate of 32.5 Mbps (MCS=7, 64-QAM, 8 MHz channel, 4 µs GI)
- Worldwide Sub-1 GHz frequency bands: 850–950 MHz
- Channel bandwidth options: 1/2/4/8 MHz
- Max output power: 8 dBm

**802.11ah OFDM PHY**

- BPSK, QPSK, 16-QAM and 64-QAM modulation
- Automatic frequency and gain control
- Forward Error Correction (FEC) coding and decoding
- MCS levels 0–7 and MCS 10
- Supports 1 MHz duplicate mode, Traveling Pilots, and Short Guard Intervals

**802.11ah MAC (WFA HaLow certified)**

- STA and AP roles
- Listen-Before-Talk (LBT) with energy detect
- 802.11 power save, fragmentation, packet aggregation
- Target Wake Time (TWT) for long battery life
- Restricted Access Window (RAW)
- Automatic and manual MCS rate selection

**Host Interface**

- SDIO 2.0 slave (DS 25 MHz / HS 50 MHz), 1-bit and 4-bit modes
- SPI mode operation supported

**Power Management**

- Power-down, Hibernate, and Active Rx/Tx modes
- Integrated DC-DC converter, 3.0–3.6V supply

**Security**

- AES encryption engine
- SHA-256, SHA-384, SHA-512 hardware support
- WPA3 with Protected Management Frames (PMF)
- Opportunistic Wireless Encryption (OWE)

### 1.3 Applications

IoT and M2M applications including: Surveillance Cameras, Cloud Connectivity, Low-power Sensor Networks, Building Automation, Asset Tracking, Industrial Automation, Smart Home, EV Car Chargers, Smart City Networks, Wi-Fi HaLow Client Adapters/Dongles, Agricultural Networks, Utility Smart Meters, and BT/ZigBee/Z-Wave to Wi-Fi HaLow Gateways.

## 3 Functional Description

### 3.1 Functional Block Diagram

The MM6108 SoC integrates a Sub-GHz RF Frontend (LNA, PA, ADC, DAC), RF PLL and Digital PLL (clocked by 32 MHz XTAL), 802.11ah PHY (FEC, OFDM, scrambling/descrambling), 802.11ah MAC with AES security, Wi-Fi HaLow API layer, SDIO/SPI Slave Interface, and a Power Management and Deep Sleep Unit.

### 3.2 Clocks

The MM6108 uses a 32 MHz crystal connected between XOP and XON via a Pierce oscillator circuit. Load capacitors C1 and C2 should be high-accuracy NP0 dielectric: **C1 = C2 = 2 x CL**.

| Parameter | Min | Max | Unit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Frequency tolerance | −20 | \+20 | ppm |
| ESR | 10 | 50 | ohm |

### 3.3 Power Management

All power is derived from a 3.0–3.6V supply on VBAT\_PWR and VBAT\_AON. VDDIO must not exceed VBAT. An internal buck converter requires a 10 µH inductor between IND and VBUCK\_FB, plus ceramic decoupling capacitors at CAPR, CAPD, and VBAT\_AON. For regular boot operation, JTAG\_TRST must be pulled to GND with a 10 kΩ resistor.

#### 4.2.2 Initialization

To switch to SPI mode, the host sends CMD0 while holding CS low after power-on. See SD Specification Part E1 ("SDIO Simplified Specification," v2.00) for protocol details.

#### 4.2.3 Bus Timing

SPI clock rate supports up to 50 MHz. Timing is identical to the SDIO specification. Defaults to CPOL=0; can be configured for CPHA=0 after initialization.

### 4.3 GPIO

16 GPIO pins are high-impedance at reset and during power save modes. All unused pins should be pulled up or down to ensure the lowest sleep currents.

### 4.4 Sleep/Wake Sequencing

#### 4.4.1 Host Wakes MM6108 from Sleep

1. Driver raises the wake pin and waits 10 ms before initializing the shared bus.
2. After communication, the driver waits 5–90 ms before lowering the wake pin.
3. After the wake pin falls, the MM6108 waits a dynamic period before initiating hardware sleep.

#### 4.4.2 MM6108 Wakes Host from Sleep (Host Interface Disabled)

1. MM6108 wakes and asserts the busy pin.
2. Host receives interrupt, raises the wake pin, waits 10 ms, and enables the host interface.
3. MM6108 initiates communication immediately after asserting busy.
4. Busy drops once communication is complete; the host waits before dropping the wake pin.
5. After the wake pin falls, the MM6108 initiates hardware sleep.

#### 4.4.3 MM6108 Initiates Communication (Host Interface Enabled)

1. MM6108 was previously woken by the host.
2. MM6108 asserts the busy pin to send data back to the host.
3. The host processes the interrupt but takes no further action — wake pin and host interface are already active.
4. After communication completes, hardware sleep is initiated.

## 11 Handling and Storage

The MM6108IQ is rated at **Moisture Sensitive Level 3 (MSL3)** per IPC/JEDEC J-STD-20.

After opening the moisture-sealed bag, modules must be:

1. Mounted within **168 hours** at conditions of 30°C or less and less than 60% RH, **OR**
2. Stored continuously per IPC/JEDEC J-STD-033.

ICs exposed to out-of-spec conditions **must be baked** per IPC/JEDEC J-STD-033 before mounting. Failure to comply will result in irreparable damage during solder reflow.
