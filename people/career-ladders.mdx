Career ladders define expectations for **scope, impact, and leadership** at every level. We publish them so you can see what's required to grow.

## Track summary

We support two parallel tracks: Individual Contributor (IC) and People Manager (M). Both are equally compensated and equally valued.

| IC level | Title                       | M level | Title                            |
| -------- | --------------------------- | ------- | -------------------------------- |
| L3       | Software Engineer           | —       | —                                |
| L4       | Software Engineer II        | —       | —                                |
| L5       | Senior Software Engineer    | M5      | Engineering Manager              |
| L6       | Staff Software Engineer     | M6      | Senior Engineering Manager       |
| L7       | Senior Staff Engineer       | M7      | Director of Engineering          |
| L8       | Principal Engineer          | M8      | Senior Director                  |
| L9       | Distinguished Engineer      | M9      | VP of Engineering                |

## Engineering ladder summary

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="L3 — Software Engineer" icon="circle">
    **Scope**: tasks within a service, with mentorship.
    **Impact**: ships features defined by others. Learns codebase and ecosystem.
    **Leadership**: none required. Strong async communication is plenty.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="L4 — Software Engineer II" icon="circle">
    **Scope**: full features within a service. Limited cross-service work.
    **Impact**: owns deliverables end-to-end with light review.
    **Leadership**: pairs with L3s, leaves a project better than they found it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="L5 — Senior Software Engineer" icon="circle">
    **Scope**: a service or major feature area. Designs for maintainability.
    **Impact**: lead engineer on team-level projects. Influences quarterly roadmap.
    **Leadership**: mentors L3/L4s, drives consensus on technical decisions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="L6 — Staff Software Engineer" icon="circle">
    **Scope**: cross-service systems within a domain. Sets technical direction.
    **Impact**: designs and delivers initiatives that span multiple teams.
    **Leadership**: technical lead for several engineers; speaks for the org externally.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="L7 — Senior Staff Engineer" icon="circle">
    **Scope**: org-level technical direction (e.g., a platform area).
    **Impact**: identifies and prevents million-dollar mistakes; drives multi-quarter bets.
    **Leadership**: trusted by VPs as a co-decision-maker on technical strategy.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="L8 — Principal Engineer" icon="circle">
    **Scope**: BU-level technical strategy. Sets multi-year direction.
    **Impact**: is the named technical leader for a $100M+ business or platform.
    **Leadership**: respected industry voice; recruits and develops L6/L7s.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Promotion criteria

Promotion at Intuit is **performance + sustained operation at the next level**, evidenced by:

1. Consistent "Exceeds" or higher rating for 2+ cycles
2. A nomination case authored by your manager and reviewed by skip-level
3. A calibrated decision at the directors-level promo committee
4. Two reference engineers/managers at or above the target level

There are no quotas on promotions — but there is calibration to ensure consistency across orgs.

## Switching tracks

You can move between IC and M tracks. Common patterns:

- **L5 → M5**: most common transition, usually after running a tech-lead role
- **M5 → L6**: also common when a manager wants to focus on technical depth
- **M6 → L7**: requires a Principal-level technical case
