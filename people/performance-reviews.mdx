## Cadence

We run two formal review cycles per year, plus monthly 1:1s and quarterly check-ins.

| Cycle           | Window                | Outcome                                |
| --------------- | --------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Mid-year        | June – July           | Feedback, no comp changes              |
| Year-end        | January – February    | Rating, comp adjustment, RSU refresh   |
| Quarterly check | After each fiscal Q   | Lightweight goals review               |

## Scoring scale

Five buckets. Distribution is not strictly forced, but director-level calibration aims for the rough shape below.

| Rating                | Approx. distribution | Bonus modifier |
| --------------------- | -------------------- | -------------- |
| Exceptional           | 5–10%                | 1.5×           |
| Exceeds expectations  | 25–35%               | 1.15×          |
| Meets expectations    | 50–60%               | 1.0×           |
| Below expectations    | 5–10%                | 0.5×           |
| Unsatisfactory        | &lt;2%               | 0× (PIP)       |

<Note>
  "Meets expectations" at Intuit means **you delivered the impact your level expects**. It is a good rating, not a warning sign. We re-emphasize this every cycle because some folks read "meets" as "average" — it's not.
</Note>

## How ratings get made

<Steps>
  <Step title="Self-review">
    You complete a self-review in Workday: top accomplishments, areas of growth, peer feedback you've gathered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Peer feedback">
    Your manager solicits 3–5 peer reviewers. You can suggest reviewers; your manager can add or remove.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manager draft">
    Manager writes a draft review, suggests a rating, and shares with skip-level for input.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Calibration">
    Director-level calibration meeting. Ratings can move up or down here. This is **the** moment that matters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delivery">
    Manager delivers the rating in 1:1, then in writing in Workday. Comp letters follow ~2 weeks later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How to prepare

- Maintain a **brag doc** throughout the year. Don't try to remember in January what you shipped in February.
- Capture impact in **dollars, customer outcomes, or scope** — not just activity.
- Surface impact your manager can't see (cross-team work, mentorship, ERG leadership).
- Ask for feedback from a wider circle than just your team.

## Disagreements

If you disagree with your rating:

1. Discuss with your manager first. Most disagreements resolve there.
2. Escalate to your skip-level if needed.
3. People Ops will mediate if it remains unresolved — `peopleops@intuit.example`.
