## Initial setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Power on">
    The laptop ships pre-enrolled in Jamf (Mac) or Intune (Windows). Sign in with your `@intuit.com` Okta credentials when prompted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the bootstrap">
    On macOS, open Terminal and run:
    ```bash
    /usr/local/bin/intuit-bootstrap
    ```
    This installs Chrome, Slack, Zoom, 1Password, and the team-defined dev tools (Engineering only).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign into 1Password">
    Use your `@intuit.com` email. Recovery kit was emailed by IT Sec.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify backup is enabled">
    Check System Settings → Time Machine. Backups go to the Intuit Backup S3 bucket through the agent — no action needed if `intuit-backup` is in your menu bar.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Peripheral allowance

Each employee gets a one-time **$500 home-office stipend** (taxable in the U.S.) for monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset, chair, or desk. Submit through Workday → Expenses with receipts. See [Expense reports](/operations/finance/expense-reports).

<Warning>
  Don't expense gaming chairs (>$400), Apple Vision Pro, or anything not used primarily for work. These are kicked back by Finance.
</Warning>

## Replacement and repair

- **Hardware failure**: file a [Help Desk ticket](/operations/it/help-desk) tagged `hardware`. Loaner laptop available same-day in office hubs (MV, ATL, SD, BLR).
- **Lost or stolen**: report to IT Security within 24 hours via `it-sec@intuit.example`. We'll remotely wipe and ship a replacement.
- **Refresh cycle**: laptops refresh every 3 years automatically. You'll get an email 60 days before your refresh date.

## Returning equipment

When you leave Intuit, ship your laptop, charger, and monitor (if any) to:
