## The match

Intuit matches **dollar-for-dollar up to 6%** of your eligible compensation. There is **no vesting period** — match dollars are yours from day one.

<Tip>
  To get the full match, contribute at least 6% of every paycheck. If you front-load contributions and hit the IRS limit early, you'll miss match dollars in later paychecks. Vanguard call this "true-up" — Intuit performs an annual true-up in March of the following year, so you'll get matched eventually, but the dollars work harder if invested earlier.
</Tip>

## Contribution limits (2026)

| Limit type                          | Amount     |
| ----------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Employee elective deferral          | $24,000    |
| Catch-up contribution (age 50+)     | $8,000     |
| Total employee + employer (415(c))  | $71,000    |

The 415(c) limit is what enables **mega backdoor Roth** — see below.

## Plan administrator

Vanguard. Log in via Okta SSO or directly at `vanguard.com/intuit`. Default funds are target-date retirement funds. You can switch to a self-directed brokerage window after 90 days of employment.

## Roth vs. pre-tax

You can split contributions between Roth and pre-tax. Common shapes:

- **All pre-tax**: lowers your taxable income now. Best if you expect a lower tax bracket in retirement.
- **All Roth**: pay tax now, withdraw tax-free later. Best if you expect higher brackets in retirement.
- **Split**: hedge.

## Mega backdoor Roth

For high earners, the plan supports after-tax contributions with in-plan Roth conversions ("mega backdoor"). The mechanics:

1. Max out your $24,000 pre-tax or Roth contribution.
2. Capture the 6% match.
3. Make additional **after-tax** contributions up to the 415(c) cap.
4. Convert after-tax to Roth using the in-plan automatic Roth conversion (set once, runs every paycheck).

Talk to a tax advisor — this is complicated and your situation matters. Lyra's financial coaching benefit covers this.

## Loans and hardship withdrawals

- **Loans**: up to 50% of your balance, max $50,000. Interest paid back to yourself. Default if you leave Intuit before paying it back triggers tax + 10% penalty.
- **Hardship withdrawals**: limited to immediate and heavy financial need. Apply through Vanguard.

## Rollovers

You can roll IRAs, old 401(k)s, and 403(b)s into the Intuit plan. Use Vanguard's rollover form. We charge nothing.
