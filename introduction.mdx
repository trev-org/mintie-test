Mintie SSO lets your organization replace password-based login with enterprise single sign-on. Sign your users in through your existing identity provider, provision and deprovision them automatically, and tie API access to the same identity.

<Info>
  SSO is rolling out by org behind a feature flag. If you don't see the
  options described in these docs, ask your account team to enable
  `sso_enabled` for your organization.
</Info>

## What's available today

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Email-first sign-in" icon="right-to-bracket" href="/setup/sign-in-experience">
    Users enter their email on `/login`. We detect your org and route them to your IdP.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Identity linking" icon="link" href="/concepts/identity-linking">
    Each user is linked 1:1 to an external IdP identity via `idp_provider` and `idp_user_id`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Per-org rollout" icon="toggle-on" href="/concepts/feature-flags">
    SSO is gated by the `sso_enabled` LaunchDarkly flag, set per organization.
  </Card>
  <Card title="WorkOS under the hood" icon="layer-group">
    We use WorkOS as the IdP abstraction layer for OIDC, SAML, and Directory Sync.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why WorkOS

We evaluated Okta SDK, Auth0, and WorkOS as the IdP integration layer and chose **WorkOS**. The decision came down to native SCIM, a clean Directory Sync API, and a tenant-per-org model that maps directly to Mintie's `Organization` table. Auth0 was ruled out on per-MAU pricing; direct Okta SDK was deferred to avoid one-off integration work per provider.

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Talk to your account team">
    SSO is enabled per-org. Your account team flips the `sso_enabled` flag.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your identity provider">
    Use the admin UI to connect Okta, Google Workspace, or any generic SAML/OIDC IdP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test sign-in, then enforce">
    Verify a test sign-in works, then flip "Require SSO" to migrate active sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>
