## Principles

The Code of Conduct turns our [operating values](/culture/operating-values) into specific expectations.

1. **Honesty in all dealings.** Never misrepresent products, financials, or your work.
2. **Respect for people.** No harassment, discrimination, or retaliation.
3. **Protection of company assets.** Time, money, IP, and information.
4. **Compliance with the law.** Securities laws, privacy laws, anti-corruption laws, anti-trust.
5. **Avoidance of conflicts of interest.** When in doubt, disclose.

## Specific rules

### Conflicts of interest

You must disclose:

- Outside employment (consulting, board roles)
- Significant ownership in another company (>1% or >$25k)
- Family members at Intuit or at competitors/vendors
- Personal relationships with people who report to you (or to whom you report)

Disclose in Workday → **Conflicts of Interest**. Reviewed annually.

### Gifts and hospitality

| From / To                      | Limit                            |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| Vendors → Intuit employees     | $100 / year per vendor           |
| Intuit employees → customers   | $100 / year per customer contact |
| Government employees           | $0 (no exceptions)               |
| Holiday gift baskets to teams  | OK if shared, < $250 total       |

Tickets to events with the giver present (a baseball game with a vendor): not a gift, an "experience" — log in `compliance@intuit.example`.

### Insider trading

Never trade Intuit stock based on material non-public information (MNPI). If you have access to financials, M&A, or product launches before public disclosure, you may be on the **MNPI list**:

- Trade only during open windows
- Pre-clear all trades with `legal-tradedesk@intuit.example`
- Consider a 10b5-1 plan — see [Equity & RSUs](/people/equity-rsu)

### Anti-corruption

Intuit complies with FCPA, UK Bribery Act, and similar regulations globally:

- No facilitation payments (a.k.a. "grease payments")
- Gifts to government officials require Legal pre-approval
- Charitable contributions to government-affiliated entities: Legal review

### Use of company assets

- Slack, email, GitHub: subject to monitoring (no expectation of privacy)
- Personal use is OK in moderation (sending a quick text, browsing news on lunch)
- Side projects on company hardware: OK if not competitive with Intuit, not using Intuit IP, not on company time
