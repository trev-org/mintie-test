These five values describe how we operate. They show up in performance reviews, promotion cases, and hiring loops.

## 1. Customer obsession

We start with the customer and work backward. Every meeting opens with: "What does the customer think of this?"

**What it looks like:**
- Watching customer support tickets, sitting with sales calls, reading reviews
- Pushing back on internal-facing optimizations that don't translate to customer benefit
- Killing features that customers don't use, even when they're cool

**What it's not:**
- "The customer asked for a faster horse" — we go beyond stated requests
- Building everything anyone asks for — prioritization is part of obsession

## 2. Integrity without compromise

We do the right thing, even when expensive or inconvenient.

**What it looks like:**
- Reporting a security issue you discovered, even if it'll delay your launch
- Telling a customer "no" when their use case violates our terms
- Pulling a colleague aside to give hard feedback rather than gossiping

**What it's not:**
- Being inflexible — integrity allows for nuance
- Being preachy — we lead by example, not lecture

## 3. We Care and Give Back

We're stewards of customer trust, the company, and our communities.

**What it looks like:**
- 32 hours of paid volunteer time per year (use it!)
- Charitable match up to $5,000/year per employee
- Mentoring across teams, levels, and ERGs

## 4. Courage

We take big swings, ask hard questions, and disagree productively.

**What it looks like:**
- Disagreeing with your boss in a meeting (respectfully, with data)
- Killing a project you've been working on for a year because the data says so
- Pushing for a 10x bet over a 2x bet when both are viable

**What it's not:**
- Recklessness — courage is informed risk-taking
- Disagreement for its own sake

## 5. We Are Better Together

Diversity of thought makes our products better. Inclusion is non-negotiable.

**What it looks like:**
- Soliciting input from quieter voices in meetings
- Sharing context broadly (Slack channels > DMs)
- Decisions documented as ADRs so others can challenge them later

## How values show up

| Where                    | How                                                              |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Performance reviews      | "Values rating" section, equal weight to delivery rating         |
| Promotion cases          | Required: examples of values demonstrated at the next level      |
| Hiring loops             | Final round always includes a values fit interview               |
| All-hands recognition    | Quarterly value-based awards (peer-nominated)                    |
