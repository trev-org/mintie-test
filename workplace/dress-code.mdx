## In the office

**Business casual or better.** No formal dress code. Examples that work fine:

- Jeans, t-shirts, sneakers
- Dress shirts, slacks, skirts, sweaters
- Athleisure that's clearly intentional (not just gym clothes)

What to avoid:

- Shirts with offensive content (political, profane, harassment-flavored)
- Shorts that read as beachwear
- Visible undergarments
- Flip-flops on engineering floors (safety)

## Customer-facing meetings

Match the customer. For most B2B customers, slacks and a button-down or dress works. For executive briefings, business formal (suit, no tie).

If unsure, ask your account team. CRM activity logs often note the customer's dress culture.

## Offsites and team events

Whatever the event leader specifies. If unspecified, business casual.

## Conferences

Match the conference culture. Tech conferences (KubeCon, AWS re:Invent) skew very casual. Industry conferences (Inman, AICPA) skew business casual. Sales kickoffs go semi-formal in the evening.

## Halloween, ugly sweater, etc.

Themed dress days are encouraged but never mandatory. Costumes with cultural sensitivity issues are not okay — when in doubt, don't.

## Cleanliness and grooming

Standard expectations. The cafe is sensitive to strong fragrance — go light.
