## Default: 3 days in office

For employees within commuting distance of an Intuit hub office, the default is **3 days per week in office**, ideally Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday. The remaining 2 days can be remote.

We chose this shape because:

- 3 days is enough for spontaneous collaboration
- 2 days is enough for deep focus and personal flexibility
- Aligning Tu/W/Th creates "anchor days" — when most people are in, on the same day

## Remote-eligible roles

Some roles are **remote-eligible**: posted as such on the job board, with offer letters specifying location. Remote employees:

- Are exempt from the 3-day expectation
- Are expected to travel to a hub for **2 weeks per quarter** (paid by Intuit)
- Should overlap at least **4 hours daily** with their team's primary timezone

Remote-eligibility is **role-dependent**, not negotiable per individual. Talk to your manager if your role's classification is unclear.

## Anchor team day

Each team picks one **anchor day** — typically Wednesday — when 80%+ of the team is in office, in person. This is when planning, retros, demos, and architecture reviews happen.

If you're remote on anchor day, you join via Zoom but should not block decisions that require in-person presence.

## Exceptions

We grant exceptions for:

- **Caretaking responsibilities** (children, elderly parents, partners)
- **Medical needs**
- **Long commute** (>90 minutes one-way) without a closer office
- **Visa restrictions**

File an exception request in Workday. Manager + skip-level approval. Reviewed annually.

## Working from another country (WFA)

Up to **20 working days per year** in a country other than your home base, with prior approval. Tax, work permit, and data sovereignty rules apply.

Process:

1. Discuss with your manager 30+ days ahead
2. File a WFA request in Workday
3. Legal reviews tax/work permit implications
4. Approved? Take your laptop and go (and respect the data classification rules from [Data classification](/engineering/security/data-classification)).

## Office hot-desking norms

- Don't leave personal items overnight (you'll be told to remove them)
- Headphones = focus mode, please don't tap on shoulders
- Use phone booths for calls — open desks are not call-friendly
- Treat the cafe as the social space; treat desks as the focus space
