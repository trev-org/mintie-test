Voice of the Customer (VoC) is how we close the loop between what customers experience and what we build next. Multiple feedback streams converge into product priorities.

## Feedback channels

| Stream                   | What it captures                                          |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Support cases            | Friction points, bug reports, feature gaps                |
| In-product surveys (CSAT, NPS) | Sentiment after key moments                          |
| Community forums         | Power-user feedback, often very detailed                  |
| User research            | Moderated interviews, contextual studies                  |
| Sales calls              | What prospective customers want before buying             |
| Churn surveys            | Why customers leave                                       |
| Reviews (App Store, Trustpilot, G2) | Public, moderated for trends                   |
| Social listening         | Real-time qualitative                                     |

## Aggregation

The **VoC Platform** ingests all streams into a single Snowflake table, then layers:

- Topic classification (Genos-powered)
- Sentiment analysis
- Volume and trend
- Cross-stream linkage (does this NPS comment match a recent support trend?)

Product teams have a per-product VoC dashboard linking back to source cases.

## Acting on it

Each PM has a **standing slot** in their cycle for VoC-driven work. Common shapes:

- Bug fixes from clustered case patterns
- Quick UX improvements from in-product surveys
- New features from research-validated prospect themes
- Help content updates for friction patterns

## Closed-loop responses

For high-effort customers (especially those who took time to write detailed feedback):

- We respond within 5 business days
- We provide context on what we heard and any next-step
- For accepted feature requests, we follow up at launch

## What gets prioritized

We don't build everything customers ask for. The bar:

- Resolves a measurable friction (case volume, NPS impact, churn signal)
- Aligns with product strategy
- Has a healthy effort:impact ratio

Product leaders own these tradeoffs.
