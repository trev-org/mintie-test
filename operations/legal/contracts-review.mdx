Most contracts go through Legal. Some categories have streamlined paths.

## Routing

| Type                                | Channel                                                  |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Vendor — using Intuit MSA           | Self-serve via `legal.intuit.example/contracts`          |
| Vendor — vendor's paper             | Submit for full review                                   |
| Sales / customer contracts          | Sales Ops (`sales-ops@`)                                 |
| Partnership / BD                    | BD legal (`legal-bd@`)                                   |
| Employment / contractor             | People Ops + Legal-employment                            |
| NDA                                 | See [NDA process](/operations/legal/nda-process)         |
| Real estate                         | Workplace + Legal                                        |

## Submitting a contract

1. Visit `legal.intuit.example/contracts`
2. Pick the contract type
3. Upload the document
4. Provide context: counterparty, business purpose, urgency, dollar value, data classification of any data flowing
5. Submit

You'll get a Legal counsel assigned within 1 business day.

## Turnaround targets

| Complexity                          | Target turnaround                |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Standard MSA, vendor on Intuit paper| 2 business days                  |
| Vendor's paper, low-risk            | 5 business days                  |
| Customer contract                   | Per Sales Ops SLA (typically 3-5 business days) |
| Strategic partnership               | Custom timeline, weeks-months    |
| Negotiation rounds                  | Add 3-5 days per round           |

## What slows things down

- Vague business purpose ("vendor for stuff")
- Missing data on what data will flow
- Missing dollar value or term
- Unidentified compliance implications (HIPAA, PCI, government, regulated finance)
- Late "just one more change" requests after contract is locked

## Self-serve options

For low-risk vendor purchases on Intuit's standard paper, you can self-serve:

- Software with standard MSA + DPA
- Service contracts < $25K, no data flow
- Conference sponsorships

The portal walks you through 8-10 questions and either issues the contract or routes to Legal.

## Confidentiality during negotiation

Contract drafts are **internal** — don't share outside the deal team. If a vendor asks about another customer or pricing, route to Legal first.

## Signature authority

Only authorized signatories execute contracts on behalf of Intuit. The list lives at `legal.intuit.example/signatories`. Common authorities:

- Up to $50K: Director
- Up to $250K: VP
- Up to $5M: SVP
- > $5M: CFO or CEO

Don't sign a contract you're not authorized to. It can void the contract or create personal liability.
