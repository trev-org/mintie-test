Intuit operates under multiple privacy regimes simultaneously. The strictest applicable rules apply.

## Applicable regulations

| Regulation                | Geography             | Highlights                                                |
| ------------------------- | --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| GDPR                      | EU/UK customers       | Legal basis required, DSR rights, breach notification     |
| CCPA / CPRA               | California consumers  | Sale opt-out, deletion right, sensitive PI category       |
| PIPEDA                    | Canada                | Reasonable purpose, consent, breach notification           |
| Australia Privacy Act     | Australia             | APP principles, breach notification                        |
| India DPDP Act            | India                 | Notice, consent, data fiduciary obligations                |
| GLBA                      | U.S. financial data   | Privacy notices, safeguards rule                           |
| FCRA                      | U.S. credit data      | Permissible purpose for credit reports                     |

## Data subject requests (DSR)

Customers can request access, correction, deletion, or export of their data. We handle these via the **Privacy Center** at `privacy.intuit.com`.

| Right          | Timeline                  | Notes                                            |
| -------------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Access         | 30 days (45 with extension)| Provide a copy of personal data                  |
| Correction     | 30 days                    | Update inaccurate data                           |
| Deletion       | 30 days                    | Delete unless retention required (tax records, etc.) |
| Portability    | 30 days                    | Machine-readable export                          |
| Opt-out (CCPA) | 15 days                    | Stop "sale" of personal information              |

The DSR backend is a coordinated workflow across products. Each service has a `dsr-handler` that performs lookup, export, or deletion in its data stores. See [Data classification](/engineering/security/data-classification) for retention rules that override deletion in some cases.

## Breach notification

If we suffer a personal-data breach:

- **GDPR**: notify Supervisory Authority within 72 hours; notify data subjects without undue delay if high risk
- **California**: notify affected residents and AG within stated timelines
- **Other states / countries**: per local law

The IR team coordinates notifications. See [Incident response](/engineering/security/incident-response).

## Privacy by design

Engineering teams must:

- Run a **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)** for new flows touching personal data
- Choose minimum data needed (data minimization)
- Default to short retention; extend with justification
- Encrypt at rest and in transit
- Tag schema columns with classifications
- Build DSR handlers as part of the service, not bolted on

PIAs go through `privacy@intuit.example` for sign-off.

## Cookie / tracking compliance

- Cookie banner served per region (EU/UK/CA opt-in; US largely opt-out)
- Strictly-necessary vs. analytics vs. advertising buckets distinct
- Updates require Privacy + Legal review
- Honor browser DoNotTrack and Global Privacy Control where applicable

## Privacy training

Required annually for all employees. Engineers and data-handling roles get an extended track.
