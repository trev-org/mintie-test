NDAs are the most common contract Intuit signs. We have a streamlined process.

## When you need an NDA

- Before a vendor demo where they'll see Intuit data or roadmap
- Before a partnership conversation involving non-public information
- Before a customer beta program
- Before sharing confidential roadmap with an investor / analyst
- Before discussing a potential acquisition or hire-out

## When you don't need an NDA

- Public-information-only conversations
- Standard sales calls (you're not sharing confidential info)
- Open-source community discussions
- Conference talks

## Self-serve NDA

For most NDAs, use the self-serve portal:

`legal.intuit.example/nda` → pick mutual or one-way → fill in counterparty info → DocuSign auto-routes.

Standard turnaround: under 1 hour for counterparty signature, often within 15 minutes.

## When to involve Legal

Use the full review path if:

- Counterparty wants to use their own NDA
- The conversation involves PII or customer data
- The counterparty is a competitor
- Term is longer than 3 years
- The counterparty is in a sanctioned jurisdiction

## What our NDA covers

The standard mutual NDA covers:

- 3-year confidentiality term
- Standard exclusions (independently developed, public knowledge, etc.)
- No-poaching language (only for senior-level conversations)
- Governing law (California for U.S.-based; varies internationally)
- No publicity provision
- Survival of obligations after termination

## Marking confidentiality

Even with an NDA, mark documents you share:

- "Confidential — for [counterparty name] only"
- "Do not distribute"

This helps in the rare case of a dispute.

## Customer / partner confidential information

When *they* share confidential info with us under NDA:

- Treat it like Intuit Confidential
- Limit access to those who need it
- Don't mix it with our own work product (so we can return / destroy on request)
- Track receipt — if asked to destroy, we need to actually do that
