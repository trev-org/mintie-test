Procurement covers anything Intuit pays a vendor for, from a $5K seat license to multi-million-dollar AWS commits.

## When to involve procurement

| Spend                    | Path                                          |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| < $1,000 / one-time      | Corporate card + expense report               |
| $1,000 - $5,000          | Corporate card; flag in expense if recurring  |
| $5,000 - $25,000         | Procurement-managed PO                        |
| > $25,000                | Procurement-managed RFP or sole-source        |
| Multi-year commitment    | Procurement + Finance + Legal                 |
| Customer data flowing    | + Privacy + Security review                   |

## PO workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit intake">
    Procurement intake form: what you need, why, vendor (if known), urgency, expected spend.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Vendor due diligence">
    For new vendors: financial health, security review, privacy review, compliance attestations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Negotiate">
    Procurement leads negotiation. They'll loop you in on scope and SLAs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contract">
    Legal reviews. See [Contracts review](/operations/legal/contracts-review).
  </Step>
  <Step title="PO issued">
    PO sent to vendor; vendor invoices against PO.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receipt">
    You confirm receipt of goods/services in Workday; AP pays.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Plan ahead

- New vendor: 4-6 weeks
- Existing vendor, new use: 2-3 weeks
- Existing vendor, existing terms: < 1 week

If you need something faster, talk to procurement; rush options exist for genuinely urgent needs but aren't free.

## Approved-vendor list

The catalog of pre-approved vendors is at `procurement.intuit.example/vendors`. Using an approved vendor cuts most of the due-diligence step.

## Sustainability and DEI commitments

- We track vendor diversity (women-owned, minority-owned, veteran-owned, etc.) and report annually
- Carbon-intensive purchases require procurement to surface alternatives
- Conflict-of-interest disclosure required for any employee with a financial interest in a vendor

## Renewal calendar

Don't be surprised by auto-renewals:

- Procurement maintains a renewal calendar
- Owners get 90, 60, 30-day warnings
- Negotiate before the renewal window — renegotiating after auto-renewal is harder
