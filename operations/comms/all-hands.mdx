We run several recurring all-hands at different scopes. They're how we stay aligned across thousands of people without it turning into chaos.

## Cadences

| Meeting               | Cadence       | Audience                  | Length |
| --------------------- | ------------- | ------------------------- | ------ |
| Company All-Hands     | Quarterly     | Everyone                  | 90 min |
| ELT Live Q&A          | Monthly       | Everyone                  | 30 min |
| Segment Town Halls    | Monthly       | BU employees              | 60 min |
| Engineering All-Hands | Bi-weekly     | All eng                   | 45 min |
| Function-specific     | Varies        | Function (Design, PM, Sec)| 30-45  |
| Team standup / weekly | Weekly        | Team                      | 15-30  |

## Company All-Hands format

A typical 90 minutes:

- 5 min: CEO opener
- 15 min: Quarter recap (highlights, lowlights, customer stories)
- 15 min: Strategic update (one of the priorities — see [About Intuit](/company/about-intuit))
- 15 min: Product showcase (a recently shipped feature)
- 10 min: Recognition (Values awards)
- 25 min: Q&A (live + submitted)
- 5 min: Close

## Q&A norms

- Anonymous Q&A welcome (Pigeonhole)
- Hard questions are rewarded — softballs are not
- Executives don't dodge — if we can't answer (legal/MNPI), we say "can't share now and here's why"
- Top-voted questions go first

## Recordings

All hands are recorded and posted within 24h. Internal-only; never share externally.

## Town halls and skip-levels

In addition to All-Hands:

- **Town halls** at the segment / BU level — more focused on operational topics
- **Skip-level Q&As** — informal, with a director or VP, smaller audience, fewer rules

## What good looks like at our all-hands

- Show, don't tell — demos beat slides
- Customer stories every time
- One hard truth per meeting (we say what's not going well)
- < 30% of the time on slides; > 70% on conversation, demos, and Q&A
- Calendar holds posted 4 weeks ahead with timezone considerations
