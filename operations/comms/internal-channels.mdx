We're a Slack-first company with email for formal stuff and Confluence for durable docs. (We're moving from Confluence to Mintlify for engineering docs — see this site.)

## Slack

Default channels everyone joins:

- `#announcements` — company-wide, low traffic, read-only
- `#new-hires` — say hi, ask anything, no judgment
- `#tech-news` — engineering all-hands news
- `#intuit-life` — culture, photos, fun
- `#asks` — generic asks for help across teams

Etiquette:

- Use threads. Channel-pollution is a fire-able offense (kidding, mostly)
- Default to public channels. Private DMs hide context from people who could help
- Avoid `@channel` and `@here` unless genuinely urgent — these notify everyone
- Working hours indicators on; respect others' set hours

Channel naming:

- `#team-<name>` — team channels
- `#product-<product>` — product channels
- `#oncall-<service>` — paging channels
- `#hello-<topic>` — interest groups

## Email

Used for:

- Formal announcements (HR, Legal, Compliance)
- External communication
- Documenting decisions for audit purposes
- Urgent reach when Slack is wrong (e.g., personal email for laptop trouble)

You should be able to be effective with **< 30 minutes of email per day** as a non-people-manager.

## Meetings

- Default to **30 minutes**
- Default to **async first** — agenda + doc, comments, sync if needed
- Cameras on for collaborative meetings, optional for status updates
- Recording for anyone who couldn't join in their timezone
- No-meeting Wednesdays for engineers (recommended, varies by team)

## Working with global teams

Half of Intuit is outside Pacific Time. Be mindful:

- Default async
- Don't schedule recurring meetings outside someone's working hours without rotation
- Use Loom or Zoom recordings to deliver context async
- Time zones in your Slack profile and calendar
