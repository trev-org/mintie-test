You're not authorized to speak on behalf of Intuit unless designated. This page explains what's allowed and how to handle inbound asks.

## What you can do without approval

- Personal posts about your role and what you're working on, **as long as**:
  - You don't share unannounced product details
  - You don't share financial information
  - You're clear it's your views, not Intuit's
- Conference talks on broadly known topics — but pre-flight slides with `comms@intuit.example` for any product specifics

## What requires approval

Any of the following routes through Communications:

- Press / journalist interviews
- Analyst conversations (Gartner, Forrester, etc.)
- Podcasts where you speak about Intuit
- Public blog posts about Intuit work
- Conference talks with Intuit-specific content
- Media inquiries about incidents, layoffs, executive changes, financial results

Reach out at `comms@intuit.example`. Usually a same-day or next-day response.

## Inbound from a journalist

If a journalist reaches you directly:

1. Don't respond on substance — even casually
2. Forward to `comms@intuit.example`
3. Comms decides whether to engage, who speaks, on what terms

This applies whether the inquiry is about your team, a competitor, or "industry trends." Even seemingly innocuous quotes can be problematic, especially during financial quiet periods.

## Quiet periods

In the **2 weeks before earnings** through the earnings call, we observe a quiet period:

- No new public statements about company performance
- No analyst meetings on company specifics
- Marketing announcements continue but are vetted

Watch for the quiet-period notification in `#announcements` quarterly.

## Investor relations

All communications with current or prospective investors go through Investor Relations (`ir@intuit.example`). This includes:

- Calls from analysts
- Inquiries from buy-side / sell-side
- Conference invitations from investor-focused events

## Social media

- Personal accounts: yours, please be thoughtful
- "About me" can mention you work at Intuit
- Don't reply to customer complaints publicly — direct them to support
- For Intuit-branded social accounts, only the social media team posts

## Crisis comms

If something happens that could become a media story (incident, layoffs leaked, regulatory action):

- Don't speculate publicly
- Don't post about it on social media
- Comms team and legal lead the response
- We'll communicate to employees through normal channels (announcements, all-hands)
